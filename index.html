Receiving language as input

Soundwaves that are reeived are intially seperated by frequency in the inner ear. These frequencies are mappedalong the basilar membrane in our cochlea. These frequencies are converted as activity in the cochlear nerve fibers, which terminate in the cochlear nuclear complex in the brain.
